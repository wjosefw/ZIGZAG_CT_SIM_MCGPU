THREE COORDINATE BUGS FOUND AND FIXED
--------------------------------------

1. X AXIS — Phantom is on the DETECTOR side of the isocenter
   Expected (naive): phantom at X=[0,15] (between isocenter and source)
   Actual:           phantom at X=[-15,0] (between isocenter and detector)

   MC-GPU places the phantom "lower back corner" at X=0 (isocenter / chest wall)
   with the phantom extending in the -X direction toward the detector/nipple.

   Fix: img_origin_x = -14.95  (center of voxel ix=0 is at world X=-14.95)
   Phantom spans X=[-14.95, -0.05] in parallelproj coords.

2. Y AXIS — MC-GPU voxel Y index is INVERTED relative to parallelproj
   MC-GPU: iy=0 corresponds to MAXIMUM physical Y (Y = oy + (Ny-1)*dy = 14.9 cm)
   parallelproj: index 0 corresponds to minimum coordinate.

   Fix: np.flip(point_img, axis=1).copy() before passing to parallelproj.
   img_origin_y = 0.05 (standard: oy + dy/2, correct AFTER the flip).

   After flip, sphere center moves from voxel j=75 to j=74 (149-75=74),
   mapping to world Y = 0.05 + 74*0.1 = 7.45 cm. ✓

3. Z AXIS — Phantom bottom is NOT at Z=0
   The phantom's "lower back corner" ends up at world Z≈5.5 cm, not Z=0.
   The exact reason is unclear (possibly the missing .in file had a non-zero
   Z phantom offset, or MC-GPU uses a different Z origin convention).
   Determined empirically from ray intersection analysis.

   Fix: img_origin_z = 5.55  (center of voxel iz=0 is at world Z=5.55)
   Phantom spans Z=[5.55, 20.45] in parallelproj coords.

HOW THE LISTMODE DATA IS STRUCTURED
-------------------------------------
  y = ln(blank_signal / phantom_signal), clipped to >= 0
  y > 0: rays that passed through attenuating material
  y = 0: rays through air (blank == phantom, or phantom brighter due to noise)

  xstart = source focal spot position (from MC-GPU header, exact)
  xend   = detector pixel center (computed from SDD, detector size, rotation)

  The blank scan is used for geometric normalization of the fan/cone beam.
  It does NOT represent a material reference — it represents the unattenuated beam.


MLEM RECONSTRUCTION (sim_mlem cell)
--------------------------------------
  mask = y > 0  (only rays with measured attenuation)
  sensitivity = A^T * 1  (backproject ones to get normalization)
  x initialized to 0.2 cm^-1 (mu_water at ~100 kVp)
  10 iterations of:  x ← x * (A^T (y / Ax)) / sensitivity

  Known limitation: beam is polychromatic (100 kVp), model is monochromatic.
  Scatter is included in the signal (Build_listmode reads scatter+primaries).
  Both cause beam hardening / model mismatch artifacts.
